Ada Lovelace

From Biography.com

A gifted mathematician, Ada Lovelace is considered to have writeen instructions for the first computer program in the mid-1800s.

Background

The daughter of famed poet Lord Byron, Augusta Ada Byron, Countess of Lovelace-- better known as 'Ada Lovelace'-- was born in London on December 10, 1815.

Major Accomplishments

Around the age of 17, Ada met Charles Babbage, a mathematician and inventor, known as the father of the computer. Ada was asked to translate on Babbage's difference engine, a machine that was meanth to perform mathematical calculations. She added her own thoughts and ideas on the machine and offered up other forward-thinking concepts.

For her work, Ada is often considered to be the first computer programmer.
